About design roles

Digital teams depend on designers to help shape the look and feel of a service.

They work with user researchers to understand how users interact with a service. They take account of what motivates users to use a service and what their current experience is. They look for what is causing users pain. Good service design provides a user experience(UX) that meets their needs and expectations.

There are different design roles with a digital team.

Service designers consider the entire end-to-end service. From when a user starts their journey to when they complete it.

They look for opportunities to create a simple but effective user journey. Good service design will minimise the burden on the user. It will provide a smooth and intuitive user experience that avoids confusion and frustration.

They test their service design with real users to get feedback. The feedback helps to iterate the service further, making it better each time.

Interaction designers (also known as 'UX designers' or 'visual designers') help develop design concepts. i.e. pages/screens.

These are shared with users to test and seek feedback on. Their outputs are sometimes known as 'mock-ups' or 'wireframes'. These enable users to test the look and feel of a service design before it coding by developers. The feedback helps to iterate the design further.

They work with front end developers to develop coded versions of the design. These versions are further tested and will form part of the live service.
